# TAMSK — Official Rules

Source: https://www.gipf.com/tamsk/rules/rules.html

## Overview

A territorial game with time as a special feature! TAMSK is a two-player strategic game where players compete to place rings on the board by moving hourglasses.

## Objective

The player with the fewest rings remaining (i.e., who placed the most rings) at the end of the game is the winner.

## Components

- Game board with 37 spaces of varying heights (ring capacities)
- 6 hourglasses (3 per player, each a 3-minute sand timer)
- 1 neutral 15-second pressure timer
- 64 ivory rings (32 per player)
- 2 ring holders

## Board Layout

Spaces have different ring capacities based on distance from center:
- Center space: 4 rings
- Inner ring (distance 1): 3 rings each
- Middle ring (distance 2): 2 rings each
- Outer ring (distance 3): 1 ring each

Hourglasses start on the 6 corner positions (distance 3), alternating colors.

## Gameplay

### Moving

On each turn, a player must move one of their hourglasses to any adjacent vacant space. A space is "vacant" if:
- No other hourglass occupies it
- It has not reached its maximum ring capacity

### Placing Rings

After moving an hourglass, the player MAY place one ring from their supply onto the space where the hourglass landed. This is optional.

If a player skips placing a ring, their opponent may place one of THEIR OWN rings on that space instead (also optional).

### Passing

A player must pass their turn when all of their hourglasses are blocked — meaning none can move to any adjacent vacant space. This happens when hourglasses are surrounded by other hourglasses and/or spaces at maximum ring capacity.

When a player must pass, the pass happens automatically (the player cannot delay to consume opponent's timer).

### Game End

The game ends when:
- Both players must pass consecutively (neither can move)
- All rings have been placed
- (Levels 2/3) All hourglasses of both players have run out

## Three Difficulty Levels

### Level 1 — No Time Pressure

Hourglasses are moved as normal pieces without flipping timers. Pure strategy, no time pressure.

### Level 2 — Timers Active

- During the first 3 turns, each player must move each of their 3 hourglasses once (one per turn), flipping it to start its timer.
- After the first 3 turns, each turn the player must flip (reverse) one of their hourglasses when moving it.
- When an hourglass runs out of sand, it is "dead" — it cannot be moved again but remains on the board as an obstacle.
- A player whose hourglass runs out loses that hourglass for movement.
- Tiebreaker: if both players have placed the same number of rings, the player with the last surviving hourglass wins.

### Level 3 — Full Game (with 15-Second Pressure Timer)

All Level 2 rules apply, plus:

- Either player may activate the 15-second pressure timer during their opponent's turn to force faster play.
- If the opponent fails to complete their move within 15 seconds after the pressure timer is activated, they suffer a penalty: the activating player may place 2 rings on their next turn instead of 1.
- If the opponent hasn't even started their move when the timer expires, they forfeit that turn entirely (same penalty applies).

## Winning

- **Standard victory:** The player with fewer remaining rings (who placed more rings) wins.
- **Level 1 ties:** A draw is possible when ring counts are equal.
- **Level 2/3 ties:** Resolved by hourglass survival — the player with a surviving hourglass wins.
